History

Water rafting, originating in the mid-19th century for transportation and exploration, transformed into a thrilling recreational activity by the mid-20th century. The first commercial rafting trips began in the 1950s in the United States, with the Grand Canyon becoming a popular destination.

As equipment and safety standards improved, rafting spread globally, attracting adventure seekers to navigate rivers' rapids and enjoy breathtaking scenery. Rafting competitions emerged, further popularizing the sport. Today, water rafting is a beloved outdoor adventure activity, offering adrenaline-pumping experiences and unforgettable journeys through some of the world's most picturesque and challenging river landscapes.
